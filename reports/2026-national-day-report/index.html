The 2026 National Day Report: 743 Observances, Fully Re-Audited

Originally published February 21, 2026 · Updated and re-audited July 16, 2026 · Source: holidays.json in this repository, cross-checked entry by entry.

⚠️ Why we rebuilt this report from scratch

While auditing the underlying dataset for duplicate entries, we found that the February edition's category model had a bug: any observance that didn't match a specific food, awareness, or lifestyle keyword was silently dumped into "Animals & Nature," inflating it to a fabricated 37.16% "lead" it didn't earn. Spot-checking that bucket, roughly 8 in 10 of its entries — things like Static Electricity Day, National Periodic Table Day, and Dr. Seuss Day — had nothing to do with animals or nature at all.

We rebuilt the categorization from the ground up with a wider, more specific keyword model and an honestly-labeled catch-all bucket (see Methodology). The numbers below are not a light refresh of the old ones — several headline claims have changed because the old ones were wrong, not just stale.

Executive Summary

  • The dataset now covers 743 observances — 732 tied to a fixed calendar date, plus 11 that move every year (like Thanksgiving), tracked separately for the first time.
  • Food & Drink is the true largest category at 37.55% (279 of 743) — not the 37.16% "Animals & Nature" claimed in February, which was a mislabeled catch-all bucket.
  • Properly measured, Animals & Nature is 10.09% (75 of 743) of the dataset — a fifth of its previously reported size.
  • Food is not just dessert: 63.1% of food entries are non-dessert (savory, beverage, produce, or other).
  • Across all 743 entries, the 🍪 cookie emoji is used more than any other (17 times), and the average observance carries 3.65 fun facts.
  • Since February, we corrected 9 duplicate-listing errors in the live database — see the full changelog below.

What Changed Since the February Edition

This report doesn't just refresh numbers on a schedule — the underlying dataset is corrected in the open whenever we catch a problem, and this edition reflects nine specific fixes made between February and July. Each was a case where the database briefly listed the same holiday twice under near-identical names (e.g., "Pecan Pie Day" and "National Pecan Pie Day" for the same date) — a duplicate-detection audit caught all nine, and each was replaced with a distinct, real observance rather than deleted.

Food Observance Analysis

Food-related observances represent 37.55% (279 of 743) of the dataset, making Food & Drink the single largest category. Splitting food entries into subtypes shows the set is broader than dessert alone: Dessert & Sweets is the largest subtype at 103 entries (36.9%), but Other Food (73, 26.2%), Savory Meals (42, 15.1%), Beverages (34, 12.2%), and Produce & Ingredients (27, 9.7%) combine for 63.1% of food entries.

"Nearly two-thirds of food-related observances have nothing to do with dessert."

For editorial planning, this spread supports dessert content for seasonal baking periods, beverage content for quick social formats, and savory or produce content for utility-first recipe posts — the food lane is not a one-note dessert blog.

Emoji Analysis

Every observance in the dataset carries an emoji. Across all 743 entries, 403 unique emoji are used — but a handful dominate. The 🍪 cookie emoji leads with 17 uses, followed by 🍫 chocolate bar (15) and 🍰 cake slice (11), reinforcing the food-heavy skew seen throughout this report. No observance is missing an emoji.

"The cookie emoji appears on 17 different national-day observances — more than any other symbol in the dataset."

Naming Patterns

How an observance is named turns out to be fairly predictable: 52.5% (390 of 743) use a plain name with no prefix (e.g. "Groundhog Day"), while 39.7% (295) start with the word "National." "World" (30, 4.0%) and "International" (28, 3.8%) prefixes are comparatively rare, typically reserved for observances with a genuine global framing rather than a U.S.-centric one.

The Obscurity Index

The Obscurity Index is a practical proxy for lexical specificity built from three signals: the longest observance names, niche-themed wording, and hobby-oriented themes. Longer, more specific names tend to indicate narrower search intent — useful for long-tail content planning.

Longest names in the current dataset include "National Pizza with the Works Except Anchovies Day" (50 characters), "Answer The Telephone Like Buddy The Elf Day" (43), and "National Law Enforcement Appreciation Day" (41). Niche-themed examples (11 matched) include Morse code, cellophane tape, plimsoll, and barbershop quartet. Hobby-themed examples (20 matched) include photography, puzzles, kite flying, and crossword-adjacent wordplay.

Methodology

  • Dataset source: holidays.json snapshot in this repository, covering both the fixed 366-day calendar and the separate floating-date list.
  • Total records analyzed: 743 (732 fixed-date + 11 floating-date).
  • Categories are assigned by checking each observance's name against ten ordered keyword rule sets (Food & Drink, Animals & Nature, Kindness & Relationships, Wellness & Self-Care, Awareness & Advocacy, Science/History & Curiosities, Work & Technology, Arts & Culture, Seasonal & Calendar), first match wins. Anything matching none of these is labeled "Fun & Weird (uncategorized)" — honestly, rather than folded into an unrelated category.
  • This replaces the February edition's model, which used "Animals & Nature" as an undisclosed default bucket for anything unmatched, inflating its share to 37.16%.
  • Food sub-breakdown uses a second keyword pass against food-category names only (Dessert & Sweets, Savory Meals, Beverages, Produce & Ingredients, Other Food).
  • Emoji and fun-fact statistics are read directly from each record's stored fields; no inference involved.
  • Obscurity index uses character-length ranking plus a curated niche/hobby keyword list, disclosed on request.
  • Charts are static SVG files generated directly from the computed JSON output in the repo pipeline — the same numbers appear in the downloadable CSV/JSON.
