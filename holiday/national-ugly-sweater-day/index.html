Overview

National Ugly Sweater Day is an annual celebration encouraging people to don their most garish, brightly colored, and often handmade festive sweaters. Typically observed in December, it's a lighthearted tradition embraced by offices, schools, and social gatherings to spread holiday cheer and often raise money for charity.

Observed each year on December 19, National Ugly Sweater Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The trend of wearing "ugly" Christmas sweaters began in the 1980s, often seen as ironic fashion popularized by TV shows and movies.

The first official "Ugly Christmas Sweater Party" is often credited to a group in Vancouver, Canada, in 2002, helping to ignite the modern phenomenon.
