Overview

World Turtle Day is observed annually on May 23rd, established in 2000 by American Tortoise Rescue. Its purpose is to raise awareness about turtles and tortoises and encourage human action to help them survive and thrive. The day promotes appreciation for these ancient reptiles and highlights the threats they face, such as habitat destruction, illegal pet trade, and climate change, while encouraging conservation efforts worldwide.

Observed each year on May 23, World Turtle Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Turtles and tortoises are some of the oldest living reptiles, with their lineage dating back over 200 million years.

A turtle's shell is an integral part of its skeleton, made up of over 50 bones including the ribs and spine, meaning they cannot leave their shell.
