Overview

Polar Bear Day, observed annually on February 27th, is an international event dedicated to raising awareness about polar bears and the critical conservation challenges they face. Organized by Polar Bears International, this day highlights the threats posed by climate change, particularly the melting Arctic sea ice, which is vital for their hunting, mating, and denning. It encourages global action and support for research and conservation efforts to protect these iconic Arctic predators and their fragile ecosystem.

Observed each year on February 27, Polar Bear Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Polar bears are classified as marine mammals due to their reliance on the ocean and sea ice for survival.

Their fur is not white, but rather transparent and hollow, and their skin underneath is black.
