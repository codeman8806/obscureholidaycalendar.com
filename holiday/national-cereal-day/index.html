Overview

National Cereal Day, observed annually on March 7th, is a celebration dedicated to one of the world's most popular and versatile breakfast foods. This unofficial holiday encourages people to enjoy their favorite crunchy, flaky, or puffed grains, often served with milk, as a convenient and beloved start to the day. It's a chance to appreciate the vast variety of cereals available, from classic oats to sugary delights, that have become a staple in countless households globally.

Observed each year on March 7, National Cereal Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The first ready-to-eat breakfast cereals were developed in the late 19th century by health reformers like John Harvey Kellogg, originally marketed as digestive aids.

The average American consumes approximately 160 bowls of cereal per year.
