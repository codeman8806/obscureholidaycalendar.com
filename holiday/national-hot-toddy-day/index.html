National Hot Toddy Day ☕️

January 11 Unofficial / Just for fun Updated 2025-12-07

This holiday is featured in the Obscure Holiday Calendar app with artwork, reminders, and daily fun facts.

Overview

National Hot Toddy Day, observed annually on January 11th, celebrates a classic warming beverage known for its comforting properties. A Hot Toddy traditionally consists of a spirit (such as whiskey, rum, or brandy), hot water or tea, honey, and lemon juice, often garnished with spices like cinnamon or cloves. Historically consumed for warmth during cold weather or as a purported remedy for common colds, it remains a popular drink enjoyed for its soothing qualities and aromatic flavors.

Observed each year on January 11, National Hot Toddy Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The name 'toddy' is believed by some to have originated from a Hindi word for a beverage made from fermented palm sap.

Historically, Hot Toddies were often prescribed by doctors in the 18th and 19th centuries as a home remedy for colds, flu, and insomnia.
