Overview

Teddy Bear Picnic Day is an informal annual celebration held on July 10th, encouraging people of all ages to gather their beloved teddy bears for an outdoor picnic. It's a day dedicated to imaginative play, enjoying nature, and creating cherished memories with these iconic plush companions, often involving blankets, packed lunches, and the company of family and friends.

Observed each year on July 10, Teddy Bear Picnic Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The day is inspired by the popular children's song "The Teddy Bears' Picnic," with music composed by John Walter Bratton in 1907 and lyrics added by Jimmy Kennedy in 1932.

The teddy bear itself was named after U.S. President Theodore Roosevelt, following a hunting trip incident in 1902 where he refused to shoot a bear cub.
