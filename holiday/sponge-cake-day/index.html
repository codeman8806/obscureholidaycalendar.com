Overview

Sponge Cake Day, celebrated annually on August 23rd, honors the light, airy, and versatile sponge cake. Characterized by its delicate texture, achieved primarily through the air whipped into eggs rather than chemical leavening agents, this foundational cake serves as a beloved base for countless desserts, including layered cakes, Swiss rolls, trifles, and tiramisu, making it a cherished staple in culinary traditions worldwide.

Observed each year on August 23, Sponge Cake Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The earliest known sponge cake recipes date back to the Italian Renaissance, with some historians tracing its origins to even older Mediterranean desserts.

Traditional sponge cakes rely on the aeration of whipped eggs and sugar for their rise, giving them their characteristic light and bouncy texture without the need for baking powder or soda.
