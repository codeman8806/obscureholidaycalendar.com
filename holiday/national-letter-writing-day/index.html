National Letter Writing Day ✍

December 7 Cultural / community observance Updated 2025-12-16

This holiday is featured in the Obscure Holiday Calendar app with emoji-style visuals, reminders, and daily fun facts.

Overview

National Letter Writing Day, celebrated on December 7th, encourages everyone to rediscover the joy and personal touch of handwritten correspondence. It's a day to slow down, connect with loved ones, and appreciate the art of composing a thoughtful letter in an increasingly digital world.

Observed each year on December 7, National Letter Writing Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Before the advent of email and instant messaging, letters were the primary form of long-distance communication.

The U.S. Postal Service processes billions of mail pieces annually, including a significant number of personal letters.

Fun facts

  • Before the advent of email and instant messaging, letters were the primary form of long-distance communication.
  • The U.S. Postal Service processes billions of mail pieces annually, including a significant number of personal letters.
  • Receiving a handwritten letter can evoke stronger emotional responses than digital messages due to its tangible nature and effort involved.
