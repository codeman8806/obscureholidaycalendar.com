Overview

National Puzzle Day, celebrated annually on January 29th, is a day dedicated to honoring all types of puzzles, from jigsaw puzzles and crosswords to Sudoku and logic games. It encourages individuals of all ages to engage in mind-stimulating activities, challenge their problem-solving abilities, and enjoy the mental benefits and satisfaction derived from completing puzzles. It's an occasion to disconnect from digital screens and indulge in an engaging and often relaxing pastime.

Observed each year on January 29, National Puzzle Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The first jigsaw puzzle was created around 1760 by John Spilsbury, a London mapmaker, who cut maps into pieces for educational purposes.

Solving puzzles can enhance cognitive functions such as memory, attention, and reasoning skills, and can also help reduce stress.
