Overview

Deviled Egg Day, observed annually on November 2nd, celebrates the iconic appetizer loved for its creamy, tangy flavor. This popular dish consists of hard-boiled eggs, halved and filled with a seasoned paste made from the mashed yolks, typically mixed with mayonnaise, mustard, and spices. A staple at picnics, potlucks, and holiday gatherings, deviled eggs are a testament to simple ingredients creating a universally appealing snack.

Observed each year on November 2, Deviled Egg Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The term 'deviled' has been used since the 18th century to describe food prepared with pungent or spicy ingredients.

A precursor to deviled eggs, involving boiled eggs seasoned with spiced sauces, was a popular appetizer in ancient Rome.
