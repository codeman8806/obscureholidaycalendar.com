Overview

National Hard Candy Day, observed on December 19th, is a sweet occasion to celebrate the enduring charm and simple pleasure of hard candies. From classic lollipops to beloved butterscotches, this day encourages everyone to enjoy the crunchy, long-lasting sweetness that has delighted generations.

Observed each year on December 19, National Hard Candy Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Hard candies have been around for centuries, with early forms being medicinal lozenges.

The process of making hard candy involves heating sugar to high temperatures, creating a glass-like confection.

Fun facts

  • Hard candies have been around for centuries, with early forms being medicinal lozenges.
  • The process of making hard candy involves heating sugar to high temperatures, creating a glass-like confection.
  • Lollipops, a type of hard candy, are thought to have been invented to make candy less messy to eat.
  • Many hard candies are flavored with natural fruit extracts or essential oils.
  • The longest-lasting hard candy ever recorded took over 7 hours to dissolve in a single person's mouth.
