Overview

Tell the Truth Day, observed annually on July 7th, is an informal holiday that encourages individuals to practice honesty and transparency in their daily interactions. While not an officially recognized public holiday, it serves as a lighthearted reminder to be truthful in words and actions, promoting integrity, clear communication, and the value of sincerity in relationships. Its origins are obscure, but its message is universally understood as a call for candidness.

Observed each year on July 7, Tell the Truth Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.
