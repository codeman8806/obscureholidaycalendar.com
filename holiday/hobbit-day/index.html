Overview

Hobbit Day, celebrated annually on September 22nd, commemorates the shared birthdays of the beloved hobbit characters Bilbo Baggins and Frodo Baggins from J.R.R. Tolkien's iconic works, *The Hobbit* and *The Lord of the Rings*. It is a day for fans worldwide to honor Tolkien's literary legacy and the charming culture of the Shire, often observed with feasts, themed gatherings, and reflections on the stories.

Observed each year on September 22, Hobbit Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The specific date of September 22nd is explicitly mentioned as the shared birthday of Bilbo and Frodo Baggins in *The Lord of the Rings*.

Hobbit Day is the highlight of "Tolkien Week," an annual celebration organized by the American Tolkien Society since 1978.
