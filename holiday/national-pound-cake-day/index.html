Overview

National Pound Cake Day, celebrated annually on March 4th, honors the classic, rich, and dense cake known for its simple yet perfect ingredients. Traditionally made with a pound each of butter, sugar, eggs, and flour, this versatile dessert is enjoyed plain, with fruit, or as a base for various toppings, making it a beloved staple in many cuisines.

Observed each year on March 4, National Pound Cake Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The name "pound cake" originated from its traditional recipe that called for a pound each of butter, sugar, eggs, and flour.

Early pound cake recipes often relied solely on the aeration from whipping eggs and butter for leavening, predating chemical leaveners like baking powder.
