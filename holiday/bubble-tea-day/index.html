Overview

Bubble Tea Day, celebrated on April 30th, honors the popular Taiwanese beverage known for its diverse tea bases, fruit or milk flavorings, and signature chewy tapioca pearls (boba). This unofficial holiday encourages enthusiasts worldwide to enjoy and appreciate the cultural impact and versatility of bubble tea, which has evolved from a local Taiwanese street drink into a global phenomenon.

Observed each year on April 30, Bubble Tea Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Bubble tea originated in Taiwan in the 1980s, with two prominent tea houses, Chun Shui Tang and Hanlin Tea Room, often credited with its invention.

The 'bubbles' in bubble tea are traditionally chewy tapioca pearls, made from cassava root, which are often sweetened with brown sugar syrup.

Fun facts

  • Bubble tea originated in Taiwan in the 1980s, with two prominent tea houses, Chun Shui Tang and Hanlin Tea Room, often credited with its invention.
  • The 'bubbles' in bubble tea are traditionally chewy tapioca pearls, made from cassava root, which are often sweetened with brown sugar syrup.
  • While 'bubble tea' refers to the foamy appearance created by shaking the tea, 'boba' specifically refers to the large tapioca pearls, though the terms are often used interchangeably globally.
