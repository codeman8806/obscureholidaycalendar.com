Overview

National Gingerbread Cookie Day, celebrated annually on November 21st, is a sweet occasion dedicated to the iconic spiced cookie. This beloved treat, known for its distinct aroma of ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and molasses, holds a special place in culinary history and festive traditions worldwide. It's a perfect day to indulge in baking, decorating, and sharing these delicious, aromatic cookies.

Observed each year on November 21, National Gingerbread Cookie Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The history of gingerbread can be traced back to ancient civilizations, with ginger being a highly valued spice brought to Europe by Crusaders.

Queen Elizabeth I is often credited with creating the first gingerbread "men" in the 16th century, said to be decorated to resemble important guests.

Fun facts

  • The history of gingerbread can be traced back to ancient civilizations, with ginger being a highly valued spice brought to Europe by Crusaders.
  • Queen Elizabeth I is often credited with creating the first gingerbread "men" in the 16th century, said to be decorated to resemble important guests.
  • Gingerbread houses gained significant popularity in Germany during the 16th century, especially after the publication of the Brothers Grimm's fairy tale "Hansel and Gretel."
  • In 2013, the world's largest gingerbread house was built in Bryan, Texas, spanning over 60 feet long and requiring a building permit for its construction.
  • The rich blend of spices like ginger, cinnamon, and cloves in gingerbread historically served not only for flavor but also as a natural preservative, allowing the cookies to last longer.
