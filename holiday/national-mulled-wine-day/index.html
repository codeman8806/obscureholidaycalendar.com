Overview

National Mulled Wine Day, celebrated annually on March 3rd, is a cozy observance dedicated to the aromatic and warming beverage. Typically made with red wine infused with mulling spices like cinnamon, cloves, star anise, and citrus, mulled wine is a beloved drink often associated with colder months and festive gatherings. This day encourages enthusiasts to gather, prepare, and enjoy a steaming mug of this spiced concoction, appreciating its comforting flavors and rich history.

Observed each year on March 3, National Mulled Wine Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The origins of mulled wine can be traced back to ancient Roman times, where they heated wine with spices to both preserve it and improve its flavor.
