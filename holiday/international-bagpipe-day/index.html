Overview

International Bagpipe Day, observed annually on March 10th, is a global celebration dedicated to the diverse history, culture, and artistry of bagpipes from around the world. It aims to raise awareness and appreciation for this ancient and complex instrument, highlighting its numerous forms, musical traditions, and the communities that keep its legacy alive through performances, education, and cultural exchange.

Observed each year on March 10, International Bagpipe Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Bagpipes are not exclusive to Scotland; variations of the instrument are found in many cultures across Europe, the Middle East, and North Africa.

The earliest archaeological evidence suggests that bagpipes may have originated in the ancient world, with references potentially dating back to the Roman Empire.
