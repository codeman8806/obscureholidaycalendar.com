Overview

National Banana Bread Day, observed annually on February 23rd, is a day dedicated to celebrating the beloved quick bread known for its moist texture and sweet banana flavor. This day encourages home bakers and enthusiasts alike to bake, share, and enjoy this comforting treat, often made as a delicious way to utilize overripe bananas and prevent food waste.

Observed each year on February 23, National Banana Bread Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The popularity of banana bread in American kitchens surged during the Great Depression as a resourceful way to use overripe bananas.

The widespread availability of baking soda and baking powder in the 1930s was crucial for the development of banana bread as a 'quick bread'.
