Overview

Let It Go Day, observed annually on June 23rd, is an informal holiday dedicated to the practice of releasing grudges, past hurts, negative thoughts, and anything that no longer serves one's well-being. It encourages individuals to embrace emotional freedom, forgiveness, and a forward-looking mindset for improved mental peace and personal growth.

Observed each year on June 23, Let It Go Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

It is an unofficial observance, primarily promoted within wellness and self-help communities.

Common ways to celebrate include journaling, meditating on release, practicing forgiveness, or decluttering physical or emotional spaces.
