Overview

National Pig Day, observed annually on March 1st, is a celebration primarily held in the United States to honor the pig as one of humanity's most intelligent and domesticated animals. Initiated in 1972 by sisters Ellen Stanley and Mary Lynne Rave, the day encourages a positive appreciation for pigs, often through events and activities that highlight their unique characteristics and contributions.

Observed each year on March 1, National Pig Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Why it matters: National Pig Day, celebrated on March 1, recognizes the intelligence and contributions of pigs, fostering a deeper appreciation for these often-overlooked animals. This observance promotes kindness and community engagement through events that highlight the unique traits of pigs and their role in our lives.
