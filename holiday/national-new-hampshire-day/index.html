National New Hampshire Day ⛰️

September 7 Cultural / community observance Updated 2025-12-16

This holiday is featured in the Obscure Holiday Calendar app with emoji-style visuals, reminders, and daily fun facts.

Overview

National New Hampshire Day, observed annually on September 7th, is a celebration of the 'Granite State,' known for its rugged beauty, independent spirit, and pivotal role in American history. It's a day to appreciate New Hampshire's scenic White Mountains, its charming small towns, and its enduring motto, 'Live Free or Die,' which embodies its commitment to liberty and self-reliance.

Observed each year on September 7, National New Hampshire Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

New Hampshire was the first of the thirteen colonies to declare its independence from Great Britain, doing so on January 5, 1776.

Mount Washington, located in the White Mountains, holds the record for the highest wind speed ever recorded on land not associated with a tornado, at 231 mph.
