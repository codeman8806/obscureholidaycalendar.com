Overview

National Earmuff Day, observed annually on March 13th, celebrates the clever invention of the earmuff and its youthful creator, Chester Greenwood. Frustrated with cold ears while ice skating, 15-year-old Greenwood, living in Farmington, Maine, designed a solution in 1873, which evolved into the patented ear-protectors that became widely popular.

Observed each year on March 13, National Earmuff Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Chester Greenwood was only 15 years old when he invented the earmuff in 1873, initially using wire, beaver fur, and cloth.

Earmuffs are the official state historical artifact of Maine, and Greenwood's hometown of Farmington annually celebrates 'Chester Greenwood Day' with a parade.
