Overview

National Rotisserie Chicken Day, observed annually on June 2nd, celebrates the universally beloved and convenient meal that is rotisserie chicken. Known for its golden-brown, crispy skin and juicy, tender meat, rotisserie chicken is a staple for quick family dinners, picnics, and meal prepping, offering a delicious and versatile option for many households due to its ease of availability and satisfying flavor.

Observed each year on June 2, National Rotisserie Chicken Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Why it matters: National Rotisserie Chicken Day on June 2 highlights the cultural significance of this convenient meal option, which has become a staple in many households for its flavor and versatility. The day celebrates not only the deliciousness of rotisserie chicken but also its role in bringing families together for shared meals and easy dining solutions.

Origin and story

Costco alone sells over 100 million rotisserie chickens annually, famously maintaining a $4.99 price point in the U.S. as a loss leader.

The rotisserie cooking method involves slowly turning the chicken on a spit over heat, ensuring it self-bastes in its own juices and drippings, which results in remarkably tender meat and crispy skin.
