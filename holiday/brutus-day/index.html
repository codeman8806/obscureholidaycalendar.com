Overview

Brutus Day, observed on March 15th, is an informal, modern moniker for the Ides of March, the date in 44 BCE when Roman dictator Julius Caesar was assassinated by a group of senators, including his close associate Marcus Junius Brutus. The event, popularized by William Shakespeare's play *Julius Caesar* with the famous line "Et tu, Brute?", has since become a potent symbol of betrayal and political intrigue.

Observed each year on March 15, Brutus Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The famous warning, "Beware the Ides of March," originates from William Shakespeare's play *Julius Caesar*.

In the Roman calendar, the 'Ides' referred to a specific day midway through the month, falling on the 15th for March, May, July, and October, and the 13th for other months.
