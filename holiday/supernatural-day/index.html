Overview

Supernatural Day, observed annually on September 13th, is an unofficial fan holiday dedicated to celebrating the long-running American dark fantasy television series *Supernatural*. This date marks the premiere of the show's pilot episode in 2005, and fans worldwide use the day to honor the series' legacy, its beloved characters Sam and Dean Winchester, iconic storylines, and enduring themes of family, sacrifice, and the battle against supernatural evil.

Observed each year on September 13, Supernatural Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The holiday is celebrated on September 13th because that was the original air date of the *Supernatural* pilot episode in 2005.

*Supernatural* ran for an impressive 15 seasons, totaling 327 episodes, making it one of the longest-running fantasy/sci-fi series in U.S. television history.
