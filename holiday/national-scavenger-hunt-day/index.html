Overview

National Scavenger Hunt Day, observed annually on May 24th, encourages people of all ages to engage in the classic pursuit of discovery. This unofficial holiday celebrates the joy of following clues, solving riddles, and searching for hidden objects or completing a list of tasks, whether it's an elaborate outdoor adventure or a simple indoor game. It's a day dedicated to playful exploration, problem-solving, and the thrill of finding something new.

Observed each year on May 24, National Scavenger Hunt Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The modern form of the scavenger hunt is often credited to socialite Elsa Maxwell, who organized elaborate parties with scavenger hunt elements in the 1930s.

Scavenger hunts can range from simple lists of items to find, to complex puzzle-solving adventures, including digital versions like geocaching.
