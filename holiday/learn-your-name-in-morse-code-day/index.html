Overview

Learn Your Name in Morse Code Day, observed annually on January 11th, encourages individuals to translate their names into the iconic series of "dits" (short signals) and "dahs" (long signals) developed by Samuel Morse and Alfred Vail. This unique observance serves as a fun and educational way to engage with a historical communication system that revolutionized long-distance messaging and remains a foundational element in fields like amateur radio and emergency signaling.

Observed each year on January 11, Learn Your Name in Morse Code Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.
