Overview

Unicorn Day, celebrated annually on April 9th, is a day dedicated to appreciating the mythical, horse-like creature with a single, spiraling horn on its forehead. It's a time to embrace the wonder, magic, and symbolism of unicorns, often associated with purity, innocence, and enchantment, inspiring people to celebrate with colorful, whimsical, and imaginative themes.

Observed each year on April 9, Unicorn Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The unicorn is the national animal of Scotland, officially adopted in the 15th century.

In medieval lore, the unicorn's horn, called an alicorn, was believed to possess potent healing and antidotal properties.
