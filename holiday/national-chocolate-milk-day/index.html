Overview

National Chocolate Milk Day, celebrated annually on September 27th, is a sweet observance dedicated to the universally beloved beverage. This day encourages people of all ages to enjoy the classic combination of cocoa and milk, whether prepared fresh, from a ready-to-drink carton, or as a homemade treat. It highlights chocolate milk's comforting taste, nostalgic appeal, and its versatility as a refreshing drink for everyday enjoyment or even as a post-exercise recovery option.

Observed each year on September 27, National Chocolate Milk Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Sir Hans Sloane, an Irish botanist and physician, is widely credited with introducing chocolate milk to Europe in the late 1600s after encountering a cocoa drink in Jamaica and adding milk to make it more palatable.

Many athletes and sports nutritionists recommend chocolate milk as an effective post-exercise recovery drink due to its ideal carbohydrate-to-protein ratio and electrolyte content, which aids in muscle repair and replenishment.
