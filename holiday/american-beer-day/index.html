Overview

American Beer Day, observed annually on October 27th, is a day dedicated to celebrating and appreciating the rich diversity and quality of beer brewed within the United States. It recognizes the significant contributions of American brewers, from large historic brands to the thriving craft beer industry, to the global beer landscape and the wide array of styles they produce. It's an occasion for enthusiasts to raise a glass to American brewing innovation and tradition.

Observed each year on October 27, American Beer Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The United States has the highest number of operating breweries in the world, with over 9,000 active breweries as of recent years.

India Pale Ale (IPA) is the most popular craft beer style in the U.S., accounting for a substantial portion of craft beer sales.
