Overview

National Caps Lock Day, observed annually on October 22nd, is an unofficial, humorous holiday dedicated to the "Caps Lock" key on computer keyboards. It encourages people to type in all capital letters for a day, often playfully mimicking the online etiquette of "shouting" or expressing strong emotions. While it's largely a lighthearted jest, it serves as a quirky reminder of a keyboard function that is sometimes overused or accidentally engaged.

Observed each year on October 22, National Caps Lock Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The holiday was reportedly created in 2000 by a programmer named Derek Arnold, who found humor in the key's perceived misuse.

The Caps Lock key originated from the 'Shift Lock' key on typewriters, designed to type entire sections in uppercase without continuously holding down the Shift key.
