Overview

National Potato Lovers Day is a time to celebrate the versatile and beloved potato in all its delicious forms. From mashed to fried, baked to roasted, this day encourages everyone to indulge in their favorite potato dishes and appreciate the humble tuber's immense contribution to global cuisine and food security.

Observed each year on February 8, National Potato Lovers Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The potato is the third most important food crop in the world, following rice and wheat, in terms of human consumption.

There are over 4,000 varieties of native potatoes, primarily found in the Andes region of South America.
