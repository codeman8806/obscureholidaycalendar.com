Overview

National Sisters Day is an annual observance dedicated to celebrating the unique and special bond shared between sisters. It's a day for siblings to honor the love, support, friendship, and shared history that only sisters can provide, often celebrated through quality time, phone calls, gifts, or shared activities.

Observed each year on August 4, National Sisters Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

National Sisters Day is an unofficial holiday, distinct from the broader National Siblings Day observed on April 10th.

While its exact origins are not definitively documented, the holiday has gained widespread recognition through social media and grassroots celebrations.
