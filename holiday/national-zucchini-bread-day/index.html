Overview

National Zucchini Bread Day, celebrated annually on April 25th, is a culinary holiday dedicated to the moist, subtly sweet quick bread that ingeniously incorporates shredded zucchini. This day encourages home bakers and enthusiasts alike to bake, share, and enjoy this versatile treat, which is often appreciated for its ability to add moisture and a hint of nutrition without an overpowering vegetable taste. It's a perfect occasion to make use of abundant garden harvests or simply indulge in a beloved baked good.

Observed each year on April 25, National Zucchini Bread Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Zucchini bread's popularity surged in the United States during the 1970s as home cooks sought creative ways to use abundant summer squash harvests.

Despite its common culinary use as a vegetable, botanically, zucchini is classified as a fruit because it develops from the flower of the plant and contains seeds.
