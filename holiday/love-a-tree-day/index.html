Overview

Love a Tree Day, observed annually on May 16th, is an unofficial holiday dedicated to recognizing and appreciating the profound importance of trees in our environment and daily lives. It encourages individuals to acknowledge the numerous benefits trees provide, from generating oxygen and absorbing carbon dioxide to offering shade, supporting wildlife, and enhancing aesthetic beauty. The day serves as a gentle reminder to care for our arboreal friends, whether by planting new saplings, protecting existing forests, or simply spending time appreciating their natural grandeur.

Observed each year on May 16, Love a Tree Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Trees are the longest-living organisms on Earth, with some species able to survive for thousands of years.
