Overview

Lost Penny Day, observed annually on February 12th, is an unofficial, whimsical holiday that encourages individuals to reflect on lost items, particularly the humble penny. It serves as a gentle reminder to pay attention to the small details often overlooked, and perhaps even to look down and discover a lost coin, or simply to appreciate the small treasures in life.

Observed each year on February 12, Lost Penny Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The cost to produce a U.S. penny often exceeds its face value, meaning it costs more than one cent to make the coin.

The common idiom "a penny for your thoughts" dates back to at least the 16th century, offering a small sum for insight.
