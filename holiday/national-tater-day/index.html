Overview

National Tater Day, observed annually on March 31st, is an unofficial but widely celebrated holiday dedicated to the humble potato. This day encourages people to appreciate and enjoy potatoes in all their versatile forms, from crispy fries and creamy mashed potatoes to baked, roasted, or chipped. It's a culinary celebration of one of the world's most beloved and economically important food crops.

Observed each year on March 31, National Tater Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Potatoes were first domesticated in the Andes region of South America between 7,000 and 10,000 years ago.

There are over 4,000 known varieties of potatoes, though only a fraction are commercially available.
