Overview

National Almond Day, observed annually on February 16th, is a day dedicated to celebrating the versatile and nutritious almond. This popular "nut" (actually a seed of a drupe fruit) is cherished globally for its distinctive flavor, ranging from sweet to slightly bitter, and its numerous culinary applications, from snacks and desserts to plant-based milk and savory dishes. The day encourages appreciation for almonds' health benefits, including being a good source of vitamin E, magnesium, and fiber, and their significant role in agriculture and the food industry.

Observed each year on February 16, National Almond Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Botanically, an almond is not a true nut but rather the edible seed of a drupe, which is a fruit with a hard, stony shell enclosing the seed.

California produces over 80% of the world's almonds, making it the largest almond-producing region globally.
