Overview

National Strawberry Shortcake Day, observed annually on June 14th, is a sweet celebration dedicated to the iconic American dessert. This delightful treat typically features layers of tender, often buttery, shortcake or biscuit, fresh sliced strawberries, and a generous topping of whipped cream, making it a beloved symbol of summer and a refreshing classic enjoyed by many.

Observed each year on June 14, National Strawberry Shortcake Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The term "shortcake" traditionally refers to a cake or biscuit made with a high fat content, giving it a rich, crumbly, or "short" texture.

Strawberry shortcake recipes first began appearing in American cookbooks in the mid-19th century and gained widespread popularity after the Civil War.
