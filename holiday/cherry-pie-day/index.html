Overview

Cherry Pie Day, observed annually on February 20th, is a delightful culinary holiday dedicated to celebrating the iconic baked dessert. It encourages individuals to bake, share, and enjoy cherry pies, often made with a sweet or tart cherry filling encased in a flaky pastry crust. This day highlights the rich flavor, comforting tradition, and nostalgic appeal associated with one of America's most beloved classic treats.

Observed each year on February 20, Cherry Pie Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Cherry pie is a classic American dessert, often associated with summer and patriotic holidays due to the peak cherry season and its vibrant red color.

While often considered American, pies originated in ancient times, with early versions found in Egyptian, Greek, and Roman cultures, though the sweet fruit pie we know today evolved much later.

Fun facts
