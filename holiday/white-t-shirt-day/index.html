Overview

White T-Shirt Day, observed annually on February 11th, commemorates the conclusion of the historic Flint Sit-Down Strike of 1936-1937 against General Motors. This day marks the agreement reached between GM and the United Auto Workers (UAW), a pivotal moment for labor rights in the United States. The "white t-shirt" often symbolizes the unity and non-violent solidarity of the striking workers who occupied factories to demand union recognition.

Observed each year on February 11, White T-Shirt Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The Flint Sit-Down Strike lasted for 44 days, from December 30, 1936, to February 11, 1937.

Its success was instrumental in establishing the United Auto Workers (UAW) as a significant power in American labor.
