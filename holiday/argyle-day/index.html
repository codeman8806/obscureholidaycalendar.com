Overview

Argyle Day, observed annually on January 8th, is an unofficial holiday dedicated to celebrating the distinctive argyle pattern. This iconic design features an overlay of diagonal intersecting lines on solid diamonds or lozenges, derived from the tartan of Clan Campbell of Argyll in western Scotland. The day encourages enthusiasts to wear argyle clothing, accessories, or incorporate the pattern into their daily lives to honor its rich history and enduring style.

Observed each year on January 8, Argyle Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The argyle pattern originated from the tartan of Clan Campbell of Argyll in Scotland.

Pringle of Scotland significantly popularized argyle knitwear, especially for golf, in the early 20th century.
