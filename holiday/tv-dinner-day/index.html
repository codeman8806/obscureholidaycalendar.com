Overview

TV Dinner Day, observed annually on September 10th, celebrates the iconic pre-packaged, frozen meal that revolutionized mealtime convenience. Introduced in the mid-20th century, TV dinners typically consist of a main course and side dishes compartmentalized in an aluminum or plastic tray, designed for easy heating and consumption, often while watching television. This day acknowledges the enduring legacy of a comfort food that offered a quick and effortless dining solution for countless households.

Observed each year on September 10, TV Dinner Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The first official 'TV Dinner' was introduced by C.A. Swanson & Sons in 1953.

The concept for TV dinners reportedly arose from a need to utilize a surplus of 260 tons of frozen Thanksgiving turkeys.
