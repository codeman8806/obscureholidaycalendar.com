Overview

World Chocolate Day, celebrated annually on July 7th, is an international observance dedicated to the enjoyment of chocolate. It is widely believed to commemorate the introduction of chocolate to Europe in the 17th century. On this day, people around the globe indulge in various forms of chocolate, share treats, and learn about its rich history and complex production process.

Observed each year on July 7, World Chocolate Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The scientific name for the tree that chocolate comes from, *Theobroma cacao*, means "food of the gods."

It takes approximately 400 cacao beans to make one pound (about 450 grams) of chocolate.
