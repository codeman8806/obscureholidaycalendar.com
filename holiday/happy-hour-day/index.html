Overview

Happy Hour Day, observed annually on November 12th, is an unofficial celebration of the popular social tradition known as "happy hour." This concept typically involves bars and restaurants offering special discounts on alcoholic beverages and appetizers during specific late afternoon or early evening hours, often aimed at attracting customers leaving work and fostering a relaxed, social atmosphere before the main dinner rush. It's a day to appreciate the opportunity for discounted drinks and camaraderie.

Observed each year on November 12, Happy Hour Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The term "Happy Hour" originated in the U.S. Navy during World War I, referring to scheduled entertainment for sailors.

The modern tradition of pre-dinner drinks and socializing before a meal gained popularity during Prohibition, when people would discreetly drink at speakeasies.
