Overview

March 29th marks the anniversary of a unique event in 1969 when the American Falls, one of the three waterfalls that make up Niagara Falls, was intentionally dewatered for several months. This was not a natural occurrence or an annual holiday, but a significant engineering project undertaken by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers to study the erosion of the cliff face and consider options for stabilization, offering a rare glimpse of the exposed riverbed.

Observed each year on March 29, Niagara Falls Runs Dry Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Only the American Falls were dewatered; the larger Canadian Horseshoe Falls continued to flow uninterrupted.

A 600-foot-long temporary rock and earth dam was constructed upstream to divert the Niagara River's flow away from the American Falls.
