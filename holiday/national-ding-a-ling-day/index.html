Overview

National Ding-A-Ling Day, observed annually on December 12th, is a lighthearted occasion dedicated to reconnecting with friends and family you haven't spoken to in a while. It encourages you to "ring up" or "ding" those dear to you, fostering connections and spreading a little whimsical joy through a simple call or message.

Observed each year on December 12, National Ding-A-Ling Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The exact origins of National Ding-A-Ling Day are a bit obscure, but it has been celebrated informally on December 12th for many years.

The day primarily encourages people to make phone calls to friends or relatives they haven't contacted in some time, literally 'ringing' them up.
