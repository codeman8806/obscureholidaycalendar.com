Overview

Bill of Rights Day is observed annually on December 15th to commemorate the day the Bill of Rights was ratified in 1791. This seminal document, comprising the first ten amendments to the United States Constitution, established fundamental civil liberties and rights for American citizens, including freedom of speech, religion, and the right to a fair trial. It serves as a cornerstone of American democracy, protecting individual freedoms against governmental overreach.

Observed each year on December 15, Bill of Rights Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Fun facts

  • The Bill of Rights consists of the first ten amendments to the United States Constitution.
  • It was officially ratified on December 15, 1791, after being proposed by James Madison.
  • The primary purpose of the Bill of Rights was to address concerns of the Anti-Federalists who feared the new Constitution did not adequately protect individual liberties.
  • President Franklin D. Roosevelt officially designated December 15th as Bill of Rights Day in 1941, during World War II.
  • Twelve amendments were originally proposed to the states, but only ten were ratified and became known as the Bill of Rights.
  • Among the rights protected are freedom of speech, press, religion, assembly, the right to bear arms, and protection against unreasonable searches and seizures.
