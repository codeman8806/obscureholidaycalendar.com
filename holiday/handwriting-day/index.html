Handwriting Day ✍️

January 23 Unofficial / Just for fun Updated 2025-12-07

This holiday is featured in the Obscure Holiday Calendar app with artwork, reminders, and daily fun facts.

Overview

Handwriting Day is an annual observance on January 23rd, chosen to coincide with the birthday of John Hancock, renowned for his prominent signature on the U.S. Declaration of Independence. Established by the Writing Instrument Manufacturers Association (WIMA), the day encourages individuals to rediscover and celebrate the art and importance of handwriting, recognizing its unique role in personal expression, communication, and cognitive development amidst the prevalence of digital technologies.

Observed each year on January 23, Handwriting Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Handwriting Day was established in 1977 by the Writing Instrument Manufacturers Association (WIMA).

Research suggests that writing notes by hand can lead to better comprehension and retention of information compared to typing.
