Overview

Peach Ice Cream Day, observed annually on July 17th, celebrates a beloved summer dessert that perfectly blends the juicy sweetness of ripe peaches with creamy, cold ice cream. This holiday encourages people to enjoy this classic treat, whether homemade or store-bought, often coinciding with peak peach season in many regions, making it an ideal way to savor the flavors of summer.

Observed each year on July 17, Peach Ice Cream Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Peach ice cream is particularly popular in the Southern United States, where fresh peaches are a significant agricultural crop and a staple in many summer desserts.

While fruit-flavored frozen desserts have ancient origins, peach ice cream specifically gained widespread popularity in the American South during the 19th century, leveraging the region's abundant peach harvests.
