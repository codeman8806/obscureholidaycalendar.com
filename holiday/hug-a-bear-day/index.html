Overview

Hug a Bear Day, observed annually on November 7th, is a whimsical holiday dedicated to the appreciation of bears, particularly the beloved teddy bear. It encourages individuals to embrace the comfort, nostalgia, and simple joy that these soft, cuddly companions bring. Whether it's a cherished childhood friend or a new plush addition, the day serves as a gentle reminder to find warmth and perhaps share a hug with a favorite bear.

Observed each year on November 7, Hug a Bear Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The teddy bear was named after U.S. President Theodore Roosevelt after he famously refused to shoot a bear cub during a hunting trip in 1902.

The most expensive teddy bear ever sold was a Steiff 'Louis Vuitton' bear, which fetched $2.1 million at auction in 2000.
