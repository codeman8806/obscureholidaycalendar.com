Overview

Spinach Day, celebrated annually on March 26th, is an unofficial holiday dedicated to honoring the versatile and nutrient-packed leafy green vegetable. It encourages people to enjoy spinach's health benefits, explore its many culinary applications, and appreciate its role in various cuisines worldwide. The day serves as a reminder of spinach's reputation as a superfood, often associated with vitality and good health.

Observed each year on March 26, Spinach Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Spinach is believed to have originated in ancient Persia (modern-day Iran) and was introduced to China in the 7th century.

The popular cartoon character Popeye the Sailor Man is often credited with boosting spinach consumption by 33% in the 1930s due to his affinity for the vegetable.
