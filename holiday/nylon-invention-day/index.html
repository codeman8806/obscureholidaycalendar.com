Overview

Nylon Invention Day, observed annually on February 28th, commemorates the pioneering creation of nylon by Wallace Carothers and his team at DuPont in 1935. This day celebrates the world's first truly synthetic fiber, recognizing its profound and lasting impact on industries ranging from fashion and textiles to automotive and military applications.

Observed each year on February 28, Nylon Invention Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Nylon was the first fiber synthesized entirely from chemical compounds, marking a significant milestone in polymer chemistry.

The initial public frenzy for nylon was immense; when nylon stockings first debuted in 1940, millions of pairs sold out within hours across the United States.

Fun facts

  • Nylon was the first fiber synthesized entirely from chemical compounds, marking a significant milestone in polymer chemistry.
  • The initial public frenzy for nylon was immense; when nylon stockings first debuted in 1940, millions of pairs sold out within hours across the United States.
  • During World War II, nylon became a critical material for military supplies, notably replacing silk in parachutes, ropes, and tire cords.
  • While often rumored to be named after New York and London, DuPont maintains that "nylon" was a deliberately coined, meaningless word.
