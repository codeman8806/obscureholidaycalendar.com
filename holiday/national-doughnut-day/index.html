Overview

National Doughnut Day is celebrated on the first Friday of June each year, honoring the Salvation Army 'Doughnut Lassies' who provided comfort and support to soldiers by serving them doughnuts on the front lines during World War I. Today, it's a popular occasion for bakeries and shops to offer free or discounted doughnuts, celebrating the beloved fried pastry and its historical significance.

Observed each year on June 7, National Doughnut Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The holiday originated in 1938 in Chicago as a fundraiser for the Salvation Army to help those in need during the Great Depression and to commemorate the 'Doughnut Lassies' of WWI.

During World War I, Salvation Army volunteers would make and serve fresh doughnuts to American soldiers in the trenches of France, providing a morale boost and a taste of home.
