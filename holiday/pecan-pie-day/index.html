Overview

Pecan Pie Day is observed annually on July 12th, celebrating the rich, sweet, and nutty dessert beloved across the United States, especially in the South. This classic American pie features a filling primarily made of pecans, eggs, butter, and sugar or corn syrup, baked in a flaky pie crust, offering a delightful blend of textures and flavors.

Observed each year on July 12, Pecan Pie Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

While strongly associated with the American South, the earliest known pecan pie recipes began appearing in cookbooks in the early 20th century, notably from Texas.

The pecan nut is native to North America, particularly the south-central United States and northern Mexico.
