Overview

National Chocolate Cupcake Day, observed annually on October 18th, is a delightful occasion dedicated to celebrating the rich, sweet, and comforting treat that is the chocolate cupcake. It's a perfect day for enthusiasts and casual eaters alike to indulge in this classic dessert, whether homemade, from a local bakery, or enjoyed in any form, often adorned with various frostings and toppings.

Observed each year on October 18, National Chocolate Cupcake Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The term "cup cake" first appeared in print in Amelia Simmons's 1796 cookbook, "American Cookery."

Early cupcakes were often baked in individual pottery cups or ramekins before paper liners became common.

Fun facts

  • Eliza Leslie's 1828 cookbook, "Seventy-five Receipts for Pastry, Cakes, and Sweetmeats," is credited with publishing the first specific recipe for "cup cakes."
