Overview

National Sticky Bun Day, observed annually on February 21st, celebrates the beloved sweet, yeast-leavened pastry known for its delectable gooey texture. These rolls are typically made with cinnamon, often include nuts like pecans, and are baked in a rich, caramel-like syrup that becomes wonderfully sticky and flavorful once the buns are inverted after baking, making them a popular breakfast or brunch treat.

Observed each year on February 21, National Sticky Bun Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Sticky buns are a classic treat associated with Pennsylvania Dutch cuisine, where they are sometimes called "schnecken," meaning "snails" in German, due to their spiral shape.

The signature 'sticky' quality comes from a generous coating of caramel or butterscotch syrup, often made with brown sugar, butter, and sometimes honey, which bakes onto the bottom of the buns.
