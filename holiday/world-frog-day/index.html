Overview

World Frog Day, observed annually on March 20th, is a global event dedicated to celebrating these fascinating amphibians and raising awareness about their crucial role in ecosystems. It highlights the significant threats frogs face, such as habitat loss, climate change, and disease, encouraging conservation efforts to protect their diverse species worldwide.

Observed each year on March 20, World Frog Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Frogs are amphibians, meaning they can live both in water and on land, and breathe through their skin as well as lungs.

There are over 7,000 known species of frogs, found on every continent except Antarctica.
