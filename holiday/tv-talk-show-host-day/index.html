Overview

TV Talk Show Host Day is celebrated annually on October 23rd, honoring the individuals who host the popular talk show format on television. This day pays tribute to the hosts who entertain, inform, and engage audiences, often becoming beloved and influential figures in popular culture. The date specifically commemorates the birthday of Johnny Carson, a legendary figure in late-night television who set the standard for talk show hosting.

Observed each year on October 23, TV Talk Show Host Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The date, October 23rd, is the birthday of Johnny Carson, who hosted NBC's "The Tonight Show" for 30 years.

"The Joe Franklin Show," which debuted in New York in 1951, is often cited as the first television talk show.
