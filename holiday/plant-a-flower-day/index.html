Overview

Plant a Flower Day, observed annually on March 12th, is an unofficial holiday that encourages individuals to celebrate the arrival of spring by planting flowers. It's a day to beautify surroundings, embrace the joy of gardening, and contribute to local ecosystems, whether by planting in a garden, a pot, or a public space, fostering appreciation for nature and growth.

Observed each year on March 12, Plant a Flower Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Engaging in gardening activities, such as planting flowers, has been scientifically linked to reduced stress levels and improved mood.

Many popular garden flowers, including pansies, nasturtiums, and daylilies, have edible petals and can be used to garnish salads or desserts.
