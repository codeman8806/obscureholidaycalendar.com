Overview

National Pepperoni Pizza Day, observed annually on September 20th, is a delicious occasion dedicated to celebrating one of America's most beloved and iconic pizza toppings. This unofficial holiday encourages pizza enthusiasts across the nation to indulge in and appreciate the savory, slightly spicy, and universally appealing flavor of pepperoni, often found as the star ingredient on countless pizza pies, highlighting its enduring popularity in culinary culture.

Observed each year on September 20, National Pepperoni Pizza Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Pepperoni is consistently ranked as the most popular pizza topping in the United States.

The word "pepperoni" is an American adaptation, not a traditional Italian word for a type of salami, derived from the Italian word "peperoni" for bell peppers.
