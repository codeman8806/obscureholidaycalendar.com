Overview

Chocolate Milkshake Day, observed annually on September 12th, is an unofficial but widely celebrated holiday dedicated to indulging in the rich, creamy, and beloved beverage. It encourages people to enjoy a classic chocolate milkshake, whether homemade, from a diner, or a fast-food establishment, honoring its enduring appeal as a sweet treat that evokes nostalgia and simple pleasure.

Observed each year on September 12, Chocolate Milkshake Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The term "milkshake" first appeared in print in 1885, originally referring to an alcoholic drink with whiskey, eggs, and cream.

The modern, ice-cream-based milkshake became widely popular after the invention of electric blenders in the early 20th century, particularly with Stephen Poplawski's patent for the beverage mixer in 1922.
