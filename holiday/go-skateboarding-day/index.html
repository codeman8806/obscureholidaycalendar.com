Overview

Go Skateboarding Day, celebrated annually on June 21st, is an official holiday recognizing and promoting the sport of skateboarding. Established in 2004 by the International Association of Skateboard Companies (IASC), it encourages skateboarders worldwide to unite, enjoy their passion, and share the joy of skateboarding with their communities through events, group rides, and general public participation.

Observed each year on June 21, Go Skateboarding Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The date, June 21st, was chosen to coincide with the summer solstice in the Northern Hemisphere, providing the longest day of daylight for skateboarding.

Go Skateboarding Day was officially recognized by the U.S. Congress in 2007 through House Resolution 1290.
