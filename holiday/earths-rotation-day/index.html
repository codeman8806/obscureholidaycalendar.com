Fun facts

  • On January 8, 1851, L√©on Foucault first presented his pendulum experiment to scientists at the Paris Observatory to prove the Earth's rotation.
  • Foucault's pendulum appears to change its plane of swing because the Earth is rotating beneath it, not because the pendulum itself is altering its path.
  • The Earth completes one full rotation on its axis approximately every 23 hours, 56 minutes, and 4 seconds, which is known as a sidereal day.
  • At the equator, the Earth rotates at a speed of about 1,040 miles per hour (1,670 km/h), while at the poles, the rotational speed is effectively zero.
  • Earth's rotation is responsible for the alternating cycle of day and night, the Coriolis effect that influences ocean currents and weather patterns, and the slight bulge at the equator.
