Overview

National Strawberry Parfait Day, celebrated annually on June 25th, is a delightful occasion dedicated to enjoying the refreshing and visually appealing dessert. A parfait, which translates to "perfect" in French, typically features layers of fresh strawberries, creamy yogurt or whipped cream, and often crunchy granola or cake, creating a harmonious blend of textures and flavors. This day encourages people to make, share, and savor this beloved treat, especially fitting for a summer celebration.

Observed each year on June 25, National Strawberry Parfait Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The word "parfait" is French for "perfect."

While the American parfait features layers of fruit, yogurt or cream, and granola, the traditional French parfait is a frozen dessert made with eggs, cream, and syrup.
