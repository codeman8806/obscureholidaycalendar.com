Overview

National Chocolate Mint Day, observed annually on February 19th, is a celebration of the delightful and refreshing flavor pairing of chocolate and mint. This popular combination is enjoyed in countless treats, including ice cream, cookies, candies, and beverages, offering a unique balance of rich sweetness and cool, invigorating freshness that has captivated taste buds for generations.

Observed each year on February 19, National Chocolate Mint Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The flavor combination is famously represented by the Girl Scout Thin Mints cookie, one of their best-selling varieties.

The Andes Chocolate Mint, a popular after-dinner confection, was first produced in Chicago in 1921.
