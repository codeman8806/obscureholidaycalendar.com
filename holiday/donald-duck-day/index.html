Overview

Donald Duck Day is celebrated annually on June 9th, marking the anniversary of the beloved character's debut in Walt Disney's Silly Symphonies cartoon, "The Wise Little Hen," in 1934. This day honors one of Disney's most iconic figures, known for his distinctive quacking voice, sailor suit, and famously short temper, who has appeared in more films than any other Disney character and remains a global pop culture phenomenon.

Observed each year on June 9, Donald Duck Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Donald Duck's official birthday is June 9, 1934, the release date of his first film, "The Wise Little Hen."

His distinctive voice was originally performed by Clarence Nash for over 50 years, from 1934 until his retirement in 1985.
