Overview

National Moonshine Day, observed annually on June 5th, celebrates the historical and cultural significance of moonshine, an illicitly distilled alcoholic beverage traditionally made in secret, particularly prominent during the Prohibition era and in regions like Appalachia. While historically associated with illegality, the day also acknowledges the craft, ingenuity, and evolving legal landscape surrounding modern, regulated moonshine production, which has seen a resurgence in popularity as a legal spirit in recent years.

Observed each year on June 5, National Moonshine Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The term "moonshine" comes from the practice of distilling the liquor under the cover of night to evade tax collectors and law enforcement.

Traditional moonshine is typically a clear, unaged corn whiskey, often earning it the nickname "white lightning."
