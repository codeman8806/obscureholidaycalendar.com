Overview

Mean Girls Day is an unofficial holiday celebrated annually on October 3rd by fans of the 2004 cult classic film "Mean Girls." The date gains its significance from an iconic scene where Cady Heron, played by Lindsay Lohan, recounts her crush Aaron Samuels asking her, "What day is it?" to which she replies, "It's October 3rd." The day is typically observed by watching the movie, quoting memorable lines, and sometimes wearing pink, a nod to the film's famous "On Wednesdays we wear pink" rule.

Observed each year on October 3, Mean Girls Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The film was written by Tina Fey, who also stars as Ms. Norbury, and is loosely based on Rosalind Wiseman's non-fiction self-help book "Queen Bees and Wannabes."

Lindsay Lohan originally auditioned for the role of Regina George, while Rachel McAdams, who played Regina, initially tried out for Cady Heron.
