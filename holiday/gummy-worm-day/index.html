Overview

Gummy Worm Day is an unofficial holiday celebrated annually on July 15th, dedicated to appreciating the iconic, squiggly, fruit-flavored candy. These chewy treats, often featuring two distinct colors and flavors twisted together, have been a beloved confectionery since their introduction, captivating candy enthusiasts with their unique shape and delightful taste. It's a day to enjoy this popular sweet in all its wiggly glory.

Observed each year on July 15, Gummy Worm Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Gummy worms were first introduced in 1986 by the German candy company Trolli.

They were created as a playful variation following the success of the original gummy bear, which debuted in 1922.
