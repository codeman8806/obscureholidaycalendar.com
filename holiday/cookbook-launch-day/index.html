Overview

Cookbook Launch Day, observed annually on October 12th, is a festive occasion celebrating new culinary publications and the authors who bring them to life. It highlights the excitement surrounding the release of fresh recipes, innovative cooking techniques, and compelling food stories that inspire home cooks and professional chefs alike. The day encourages food enthusiasts to explore new cookbooks, support authors, and embark on new gastronomic adventures.

Observed each year on October 12, Cookbook Launch Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The earliest known collection of recipes, often considered the first cookbook, is "Apicius" from ancient Rome.

Many bestselling cookbooks have launched the careers of famous chefs and television personalities, such as Julia Child with "Mastering the Art of French Cooking."
