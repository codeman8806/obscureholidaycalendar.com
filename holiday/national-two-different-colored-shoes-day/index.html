Overview

National Two Different Colored Shoes Day, observed annually on May 3rd, is a lighthearted and fun holiday dedicated to celebrating individuality and embracing differences. Participants are encouraged to wear two mismatched shoes, whether in color, style, or both, as a playful way to express self-acceptance and to acknowledge the unique qualities within ourselves and others. It serves as a reminder that it's okay to be different and to stand out from the crowd.

Observed each year on May 3, National Two Different Colored Shoes Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The holiday was created in 2009 by Dr. Arlene Kaiser.

Its primary purpose is to recognize and celebrate human individuality and uniqueness.
