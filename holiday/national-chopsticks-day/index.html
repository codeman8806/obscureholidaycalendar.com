Overview

National Chopsticks Day, observed annually on February 6th, is a celebration dedicated to the ancient and ubiquitous eating utensils used by billions of people worldwide. This day encourages appreciation for chopsticks, acknowledging their cultural significance and the skill involved in their use across various Asian cuisines, promoting their history, diversity, and proper etiquette.

Observed each year on February 6, National Chopsticks Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Chopsticks originated in China over 4,000 years ago, initially used for cooking rather than eating.

Globally, over 1.5 billion people primarily use chopsticks as their eating utensil.
