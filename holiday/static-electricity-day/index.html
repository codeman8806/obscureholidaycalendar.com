Static Electricity Day ⚡

January 9 Cultural / community observance Updated 2025-12-08

This holiday is featured in the Obscure Holiday Calendar app with emoji-style visuals, reminders, and daily fun facts.

Overview

Static Electricity Day, observed on January 9th, encourages us to explore the fascinating phenomenon of static electricity. It's a day to remember those surprising shocks, hair-raising experiments, and the invisible forces that govern the attraction and repulsion of charged particles.

Observed each year on January 9, Static Electricity Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Static electricity is an imbalance of electric charges within or on the surface of a material.

The 'shock' you feel is a rapid discharge of these accumulated charges to a ground or an object with a different charge.

Fun facts

  • Static electricity is an imbalance of electric charges within or on the surface of a material.
  • The 'shock' you feel is a rapid discharge of these accumulated charges to a ground or an object with a different charge.
  • Lightning is a massive natural display of static electricity discharging from clouds to the ground or within clouds.
  • Walking across a carpet can build up thousands of volts of static charge in your body.
  • The first known systematic study of electricity began with Thales of Miletus, who observed that rubbing amber could attract light objects.
