Overview

National Cinnamon Crescent Day, observed annually on April 10th, celebrates the delicious and aromatic pastry often flavored with cinnamon and shaped like a crescent moon. This day encourages people to enjoy these sweet, spiced baked goods, whether they are homemade, purchased from a bakery, or a unique twist on a classic crescent roll. It's a day to appreciate the comforting flavors and unique form of this popular treat.

Observed each year on April 10, National Cinnamon Crescent Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The term 'croissant' is French for 'crescent,' directly referencing the pastry's distinctive shape.

Cinnamon, a primary flavoring, is one of the oldest spices known, used in culinary and medicinal applications for thousands of years across various cultures.
