Overview

National Chocolate Pecan Pie Day, observed annually on August 20th, celebrates a decadent dessert that combines the rich, buttery crunch of pecans with the sweet indulgence of chocolate, all nestled in a flaky pie crust. This delightful variation on the classic pecan pie offers a perfect balance of textures and flavors, making it a beloved treat particularly associated with American Southern cuisine. It's a day for enthusiasts to bake, share, and savor this rich confection.

Observed each year on August 20, National Chocolate Pecan Pie Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

While the exact origin of traditional pecan pie is debated, its widespread popularity solidified in the American South, with the chocolate variation emerging as a beloved modern twist.

Pecans are the only major tree nut native to North America, and the United States is currently the world's leading producer.
