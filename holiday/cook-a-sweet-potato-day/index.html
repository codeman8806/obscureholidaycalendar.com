Overview

Cook a Sweet Potato Day, observed annually on February 22nd, encourages individuals to celebrate and explore the versatility and nutritional benefits of sweet potatoes. This informal holiday prompts people to prepare this orange-fleshed root vegetable in various ways, from baking and roasting to mashing or incorporating them into savory and sweet dishes, highlighting their delicious taste and health-promoting properties.

Observed each year on February 22, Cook a Sweet Potato Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Sweet potatoes are botanically distinct from white potatoes; they belong to the morning glory family, while white potatoes are part of the nightshade family.

They are highly nutritious, an excellent source of beta-carotene (which the body converts to Vitamin A), as well as Vitamin C and dietary fiber.
