Overview

Card Reading Day, observed annually on February 21st, is a day dedicated to exploring and appreciating the ancient practice of using cards to gain insight, guidance, or introspection. Whether through tarot, oracle, or standard playing cards, practitioners believe that card readings can offer perspectives on personal situations, potential future paths, and self-discovery, fostering a deeper connection to intuition and wisdom.

Observed each year on February 21, Card Reading Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Tarot cards, commonly associated with card reading, originated in 15th-century Italy as playing cards, predating their widespread use for divination.

The practice of using standard playing cards for fortune-telling is known as cartomancy, a method that dates back to the 14th century.
