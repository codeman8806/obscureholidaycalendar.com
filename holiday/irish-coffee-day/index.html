Irish Coffee Day ☕️

January 25 Unofficial / Just for fun Updated 2025-12-07

This holiday is featured in the Obscure Holiday Calendar app with artwork, reminders, and daily fun facts.

Overview

Irish Coffee Day, observed annually on January 25th, celebrates the iconic warm beverage that harmoniously blends hot coffee, Irish whiskey, sugar, and a luxurious crown of fresh cream. This comforting drink is cherished worldwide for its unique taste and warming qualities, offering a perfect pick-me-up, especially during colder months, and commemorates its rich history and enduring popularity.

Observed each year on January 25, Irish Coffee Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Irish Coffee was invented in the 1940s by chef Joe Sheridan at Foynes Airbase, a flying boat terminal near Limerick, Ireland, to warm up chilly American passengers.

The drink was popularized in the United States by travel writer Stanton Delaplane and the Buena Vista Cafe in San Francisco, which began serving it in 1952.
