Overview

National Great American Pot Pie Day, observed annually on September 23rd, celebrates the ultimate savory comfort food: the pot pie. This beloved dish typically features a rich, flavorful filling of meat (like chicken or beef), vegetables, and gravy, all encased in a flaky, golden-brown pastry crust. It's a versatile and hearty meal, cherished for its warmth and home-cooked appeal, especially as the autumn season begins.

Observed each year on September 23, National Great American Pot Pie Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The concept of pot pies dates back to ancient times, with early versions found in Roman and Greek cuisine where meat and vegetables were baked inside pastry.

While often associated with chicken, pot pies can feature a wide variety of savory fillings, including beef, turkey, seafood, or vegetarian combinations, typically bound by a creamy sauce.
