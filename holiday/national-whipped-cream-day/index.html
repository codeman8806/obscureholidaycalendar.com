Overview

National Whipped Cream Day, celebrated annually on January 5th, honors the light, airy, and delicious topping that enhances countless desserts and beverages. It's a day to indulge in whipped cream's delightful texture and flavor, recognizing its versatility and the joy it brings to sweet treats around the world. The date also coincidentally marks the birthday of Aaron S. "Bunny" Lapin, the inventor of Reddi-Wip.

Observed each year on January 5, National Whipped Cream Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Whipped cream was first known as 'milk snow' and was enjoyed in 16th-century Italy and France, often sweetened and flavored.

The invention of aerosol cans in the 1930s led to the creation of ready-to-use whipped cream in a can, making it widely accessible.
