Overview

National Pumpkin Cheesecake Day, observed annually on October 21st, is a delightful culinary holiday dedicated to celebrating the rich, creamy, and spiced dessert that combines the autumnal flavors of pumpkin with the classic tang of cheesecake. This day encourages enthusiasts to bake, share, and indulge in this beloved treat, often enjoyed during the fall season when pumpkins are abundant and pumpkin spice flavors are at their peak.

Observed each year on October 21, National Pumpkin Cheesecake Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Cheesecake's origins can be traced back to ancient Greece, where it was served at the Olympic Games.

Pumpkins, the key ingredient, are botanically classified as a fruit, not a vegetable, as they develop from a flower's ovary and contain seeds.
