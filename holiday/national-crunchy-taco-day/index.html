Overview

National Crunchy Taco Day, observed annually on March 21st, is an unofficial culinary holiday dedicated to celebrating the beloved hard-shell taco. This day encourages enthusiasts to savor the crispy, flavorful experience of a fried corn tortilla filled with seasoned meat, cheese, lettuce, and other toppings. It's a popular occasion for enjoying this iconic fast-food staple, whether at home, a local taqueria, or a popular chain.

Observed each year on March 21, National Crunchy Taco Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

While tacos have ancient Mexican origins, the hard-shell taco as widely known today gained significant popularity in the United States, notably popularized by entrepreneurs like Glen Bell, founder of Taco Bell, in the 1950s.

Crunchy taco shells are typically made from corn tortillas that are deep-fried until they become firm and crispy, often folded into their characteristic U-shape during the process.
