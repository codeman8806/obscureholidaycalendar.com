Overview

National Frozen Yogurt Day, observed annually on February 6th, is a delightful occasion dedicated to celebrating this popular frozen dessert. Often viewed as a lighter alternative to ice cream, frozen yogurt combines the refreshing chill of a frozen treat with the tangy, creamy texture and live cultures of yogurt, making it a favored indulgence for many, particularly when customized with an array of diverse toppings.

Observed each year on February 6, Frozen Yogurt Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Frozen yogurt first gained significant popularity in the United States in the 1970s.

Many frozen yogurt varieties contain live and active cultures, similar to traditional yogurt, which can be beneficial for digestion.
