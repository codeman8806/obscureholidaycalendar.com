Cinco de Marcho ❓

March 5 Cultural / community observance Updated 2025-12-08

This holiday is featured in the Obscure Holiday Calendar app with emoji-style visuals, reminders, and daily fun facts.

Overview

The holiday 'Cinco de Marcho' on March 5th is not a recognized or widely celebrated holiday. The name 'Cinco de Marcho' literally translates to 'Fifth of March' from Spanish, and it appears to be a mistaken reference or a linguistic play on 'Cinco de Mayo' (Fifth of May), a significant historical and cultural observance.

Observed each year on March 5, Cinco de Marcho invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

There are no official or widely observed traditions, historical events, or cultural celebrations specifically associated with a 'Cinco de Marcho'.

The name is a direct linguistic parallel to 'Cinco de Mayo', which commemorates the Battle of Puebla on May 5th.
