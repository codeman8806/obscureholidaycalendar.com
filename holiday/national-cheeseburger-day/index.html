Overview

National Cheeseburger Day is an annual celebration observed on September 18th, dedicated to one of America's most popular and iconic dishes: the cheeseburger. It's a day for culinary enthusiasts to enjoy various iterations of the classic combination of a savory patty topped with melted cheese, served on a bun with an array of condiments and toppings, often celebrated with special deals and promotions from restaurants nationwide.

Observed each year on September 18, National Cheeseburger Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

While its exact origin is debated, Lionel Sternberger is often credited with commercially serving one of the first cheeseburgers in the mid-1920s at The Rite Spot in Pasadena, California.

The earliest known trademark for the name 'cheeseburger' was granted in 1935 to Louis Ballast of the Humpty Dumpty Drive-In in Denver, Colorado.
