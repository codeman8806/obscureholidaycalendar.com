    Overview

    World Giraffe Day is an annual awareness and fundraising day dedicated to the world's tallest land animal, held every June 21 to draw attention to a decline conservationists call a "silent extinction."

    Why it matters: World Giraffe Day, observed on June 21, exists because giraffe populations have quietly collapsed while public attention focused elsewhere — a decline that's reversible only if people notice it in time.

    Origin and story

    World Giraffe Day was launched in 2014 by the Giraffe Conservation Foundation (GCF), a Namibia-based non-profit and the only NGO in the world focused solely on giraffe conservation across Africa.

    June 21 was deliberately chosen because it's the longest day (or night) of the year depending on the hemisphere — a symbolic nod to the giraffe's long neck.

      Ways to celebrate

      Giraffe populations collapsed by nearly a third in a generation, and almost nobody noticed — which is exactly why conservationists started calling it a "silent extinction."

      A decline that hid in plain sight

      Over the past 35 years, wild giraffe numbers have fallen by close to 30%, leaving roughly 140,000 giraffe remaining across Africa. Because giraffe are so visible and iconic — a staple of zoos, safaris, and children's books — most people assumed the species was in no danger, even as its real population quietly shrank. Naturalist Sir David Attenborough, featured alongside the Giraffe Conservation Foundation's work in the BBC documentary "Giraffe: Africa's Gentle Giants," gave the phenomenon its name: a "silent extinction."

      Why the date is June 21

      The Giraffe Conservation Foundation (GCF), a Namibia-based nonprofit and the only NGO in the world dedicated solely to giraffe conservation, launched World Giraffe Day in 2014. The date was picked deliberately: June 21 is the longest day (or night, depending on the hemisphere) of the year, a nod to the animal it celebrates.

      Ways to mark the day

      • Learn about GCF's ongoing conservation work across Africa.
      • Visit a zoo's giraffe exhibit and ask keepers about their conservation partnerships.
      • Share the "silent extinction" phrase — most people are surprised giraffe are considered vulnerable at all.

      Helpful resources: Giraffe Conservation Foundation, "World Giraffe Day".

      Did you know?

      Just like human fingerprints, no two giraffes have exactly the same coat pattern, which lets researchers identify individual animals in the wild.

      FAQ

      When is World Giraffe Day?
      It is observed on June 21 each year.
      What is World Giraffe Day?
      World Giraffe Day is an annual awareness and fundraising day dedicated to the world's tallest land animal. Held on June 21, the longest day (or night) of the year, the date was chosen as a nod to the giraffe's remarkably long neck. Launched in 2014 by the Giraffe Conservation Foundation, it rallies zoos, schools, and conservationists worldwide to fight giraffes' 'silent extinction' in the wild.
      How do people celebrate World Giraffe Day?
      People learn about the giraffe's "silent extinction": populations have fallen by nearly 30% over the past 35 years to roughly 140,000 remaining in the wild, and many support the Giraffe Conservation Foundation, the only NGO focused solely on giraffe conservation across Africa.
