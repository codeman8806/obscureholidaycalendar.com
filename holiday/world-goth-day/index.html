Overview

World Goth Day, observed annually on May 22nd, is an international event celebrating the Goth subculture. It provides an opportunity for goths to express their identity, share their music, fashion, and art, and educate the public about the diversity and complexity of the culture, often aiming to dispel common misconceptions and stereotypes.

Observed each year on May 22, World Goth Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

World Goth Day began in 2009 in the UK, originating from a BBC Radio 6 Music feature on Goth music.

It quickly expanded from a local UK event into an international celebration observed by Goths worldwide.
