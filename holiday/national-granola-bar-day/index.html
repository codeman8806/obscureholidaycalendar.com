Overview

National Granola Bar Day, celebrated annually on January 21st, honors the popular and versatile snack bar. Made typically from rolled oats, nuts, dried fruit, and a sweetener, granola bars are cherished for their convenience as a quick breakfast, an energy-boosting snack, or a healthier alternative to other treats, offering a wide range of flavors and textures.

Observed each year on January 21, National Granola Bar Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The ancestor of the granola bar, 'granola,' was developed as a health food in the late 19th century.

The first commercially successful granola bar is often credited to Quaker Oats, who introduced their Chewy Granola Bars in 1975.
