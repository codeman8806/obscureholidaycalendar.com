Overview

National Hot Sauce Day, observed annually on January 22nd, is a celebration dedicated to the popular condiment known for its fiery kick and diverse flavors. It's a day for enthusiasts to savor their favorite spicy sauces, explore new varieties, and appreciate the culinary versatility that hot sauce brings to dishes worldwide, from a simple dash on eggs to a key ingredient in complex recipes.

Observed each year on January 22, National Hot Sauce Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The heat level of hot sauces is measured using the Scoville Heat Unit (SHU) scale, named after its creator, Wilbur Scoville.

The chemical compound primarily responsible for the burning sensation in hot peppers, and thus hot sauce, is capsaicin.
