Overview

Napping Day is an unofficial holiday observed annually on the day after Daylight Saving Time begins in the United States. Conceived by Boston University professor William Anthony and his wife, Camille, in 1999, the day serves as a lighthearted encouragement for people to take a nap to recover from the lost hour of sleep. It promotes the benefits of napping for improved alertness, mood, and productivity, offering a gentle reminder to prioritize rest in a busy world.

Observed each year on March 11, Napping Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story
