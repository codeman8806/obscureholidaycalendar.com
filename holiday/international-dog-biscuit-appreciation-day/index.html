Overview

International Dog Biscuit Appreciation Day, observed annually on February 23rd, is a delightful occasion dedicated to celebrating the beloved dog biscuit. This day encourages pet owners to show their appreciation for their canine companions by offering them their favorite treats, acknowledging the joy and nutritional value these biscuits provide, and recognizing the special bond shared over a simple, crunchy snack.

Observed each year on February 23, International Dog Biscuit Appreciation Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The first commercially produced dog biscuit was created around 1860 by James Spratt, who was inspired by stray dogs eating ship's biscuits in London.

Early dog biscuits, sometimes called "dog cakes," were initially made from ingredients like wheat meals, vegetables, and beef blood.

Fun facts

  • The first commercially produced dog biscuit was created around 1860 by James Spratt, who was inspired by stray dogs eating ship's biscuits in London.
  • Early dog biscuits, sometimes called "dog cakes," were initially made from ingredients like wheat meals, vegetables, and beef blood.
  • Though often depicted as bone-shaped, many early commercial dog biscuits resembled crackers or small cakes, with the iconic bone shape becoming widely popular later on.
