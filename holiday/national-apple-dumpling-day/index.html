Overview

National Apple Dumpling Day, celebrated annually on September 17th, honors a beloved dessert featuring a cored apple (or apple slices) wrapped in pastry dough, baked until golden, and often served with a sweet syrup or sauce. This comforting dish is a quintessential fall treat, especially popular in American cuisine, and the day encourages enjoying this warm, spiced pastry, often accompanied by ice cream or whipped cream.

Observed each year on September 17, National Apple Dumpling Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Apple dumplings are believed to have originated in England, evolving from similar fruit-and-pastry preparations.

Early American recipes for apple dumplings often utilized cider, molasses, or brown sugar-based sauces.
