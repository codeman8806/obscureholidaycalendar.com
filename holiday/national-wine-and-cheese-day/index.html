Overview

National Wine and Cheese Day, observed annually on July 25th, is a delightful occasion dedicated to celebrating the timeless and complementary pairing of wine and cheese. It encourages enthusiasts and novices alike to explore diverse varieties, savor unique flavor combinations, and appreciate the rich cultural history behind these beloved culinary staples. It's a day for indulgence, shared enjoyment, and the simple pleasure of good food and drink.

Observed each year on July 25, National Wine and Cheese Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Archaeological evidence suggests winemaking dates back to 6000 BCE in Georgia, and cheese-making to 5500 BCE in Poland, making their combined history millennia old.

There are over 1,800 officially recognized types of cheese worldwide, offering an immense array of textures and flavors to pair with wines.
