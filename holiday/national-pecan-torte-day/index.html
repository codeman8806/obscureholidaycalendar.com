Overview

National Pecan Torte Day, observed annually on August 22nd, invites dessert lovers to celebrate and indulge in the exquisite pecan torte. This sophisticated baked good is typically characterized by a dense, rich texture, often made with ground pecans, eggs, and sugar, and may contain little to no flour, distinguishing it from traditional cakes. It's a perfect day to bake one from scratch, enjoy a slice with coffee, or explore various recipes highlighting the delicious native nut.

Observed each year on August 22, National Pecan Torte Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

A torte, derived from the German word for cake, typically refers to a rich, often multilayered cake made with ground nuts (like pecans) or breadcrumbs instead of or in addition to flour.

Pecans are the only major tree nut native to North America, primarily cultivated in the southern and central United States and parts of Mexico.
