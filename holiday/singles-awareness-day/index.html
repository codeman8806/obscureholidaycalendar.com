Overview

Singles Awareness Day (S.A.D.) is an unofficial holiday observed annually on February 15th, the day after Valentine's Day. It serves as a complementary or alternative observance for individuals who are not in romantic relationships, providing an opportunity to acknowledge and celebrate their single status, spend time with other single friends, or engage in self-care and personal interests away from the romantic pressures of the previous day. It often carries a humorous or satirical tone, but also serves as a genuine recognition of singlehood.

Observed each year on February 15, Singles Awareness Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

It is unofficially abbreviated as "S.A.D.," a playful nod to its observance directly after Valentine's Day.

Common activities include spending time with other single friends, engaging in hobbies, or treating oneself to gifts.
