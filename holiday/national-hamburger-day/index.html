Overview

National Hamburger Day, celebrated annually on May 28th, is a delicious holiday dedicated to honoring one of America's most iconic and universally beloved dishes. This day encourages people to enjoy hamburgers in all their diverse forms, from classic beef patties with simple toppings to gourmet creations, and to appreciate the hamburger's significant role in global cuisine and culture. It's an opportune time for backyard barbecues, restaurant visits, or experimenting with new burger recipes to savor this versatile sandwich.

Observed each year on May 28, National Hamburger Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The name "hamburger" is believed to derive from Hamburg, Germany, known for its Frikadeller, a fried minced meat patty.

While its exact origin is debated, many credit Louis' Lunch in New Haven, Connecticut, with serving the first American hamburger sandwich in 1900.
