Overview

Fortune Cookie Day, observed annually on September 13th, celebrates the iconic crisp cookie known for its unique folded shape and the small paper slip tucked inside. These slips typically contain a proverb, a vague prophecy, or a set of lucky numbers, adding an element of surprise and whimsy to many dining experiences, particularly in Chinese-American restaurants.

Observed each year on September 13, Fortune Cookie Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Despite their strong association with Chinese cuisine, fortune cookies originated in Japan, where they were known as 'tsujiura senbei' and appeared as early as the 19th century.

They became widely popular in the United States, particularly after World War II, when returning soldiers popularized them after encountering them in Japanese and Chinese-American restaurants.
