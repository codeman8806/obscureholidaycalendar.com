Overview

Martin Luther King Jr.'s Birthday is a federal holiday in the United States commemorating the birthday of Martin Luther King Jr., a pivotal leader in the Civil Rights Movement. It celebrates his life, achievements, and legacy of advocating for civil rights through nonviolence and civil disobedience, striving for racial equality and justice.

Observed each year on January 15, Martin Luther King Jr.'s Birthday invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

Martin Luther King Jr. was born on January 15, 1929, but the federal holiday is observed on the third Monday of January each year.

The idea for the holiday was initially promoted by the Congressional Black Caucus and supported by labor unions in the 1970s, eventually signed into law by President Ronald Reagan in 1983.

Fun facts

  • Martin Luther King Jr. was born on January 15, 1929, but the federal holiday is observed on the third Monday of January each year.
  • The idea for the holiday was initially promoted by the Congressional Black Caucus and supported by labor unions in the 1970s, eventually signed into law by President Ronald Reagan in 1983.
  • The first observance of the federal holiday took place on January 20, 1986.
  • Many states initially resisted observing the holiday, with some combining it with other holidays or giving it alternative names before widespread adoption.
  • Since 1994, the holiday has been designated as a national day of service, encouraging Americans to volunteer and improve their communities.
  • King was the youngest person at the time to receive the Nobel Peace Prize in 1964 for his fight against racial inequality through nonviolent resistance.
