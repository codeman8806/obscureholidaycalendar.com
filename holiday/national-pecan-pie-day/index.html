Overview

National Pecan Pie Day, observed annually on July 12th, is a culinary holiday dedicated to celebrating the rich, sweet, and nutty dessert. This day honors the iconic pie, traditionally made with a filling of corn syrup, eggs, butter, and pecans baked in a flaky crust. It's an occasion for enthusiasts to bake, share, and enjoy this beloved treat, which has become a staple at family gatherings and a quintessential part of Southern American cuisine.

Observed each year on July 12, National Pecan Pie Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

While often associated with Southern tradition, the earliest known printed recipes for pecan pie began appearing in cookbooks in the late 19th century.

Pecans, the primary ingredient, are native to North America, making pecan pie a distinctly American dessert.
