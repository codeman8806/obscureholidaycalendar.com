Overview

National Video Game Day, observed annually on July 8th, is an unofficial holiday dedicated to celebrating the widespread cultural phenomenon of video games. It encourages enthusiasts and casual players alike to embrace their passion for gaming, whether by playing their favorite titles, discovering new ones, or reflecting on the industry's significant technological and entertainment advancements. The day serves as a reminder of video games' impact on entertainment, art, and social interaction globally.

Observed each year on July 8, National Video Game Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The first interactive electronic game designed for a cathode-ray tube was "Tennis for Two," created in 1958 by William Higinbotham.

As of 2023, Minecraft is the best-selling video game of all time, having sold over 300 million copies worldwide.
