Overview

National Jelly Bean Day, celebrated annually on April 22nd, is a delightful occasion dedicated to honoring the small, colorful, and flavorful sugar-shelled candies. It encourages enthusiasts to indulge in their favorite jelly bean flavors, discover new ones, and share these iconic sweets with friends and family. The day serves as a sweet reminder to appreciate the wide variety and history of this beloved confectionery.

Observed each year on April 22, National Jelly Bean Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The earliest known mention of jelly beans in an advertisement dates back to 1861, encouraging people to send them to Union soldiers during the American Civil War.

Jelly beans became widely associated with Easter in the United States around the late 19th century, likely due to their egg-like shape and springtime colors.
