Overview

International No Diet Day, observed annually on May 6th, advocates for body acceptance, celebrates body diversity, and challenges the often-negative impact of diet culture. It encourages individuals to develop a healthy relationship with food, embrace intuitive eating, and appreciate their natural body shape without the pressures of restrictive diets.

Observed each year on May 6, International No Diet Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The day was founded in 1992 by British feminist Mary Evans Young, who had personally struggled with eating disorders and diet culture.

A light blue ribbon serves as the official symbol for International No Diet Day.
