Overview

Cable Car Day, celebrated annually on January 17th, commemorates the patenting of the successful cable car system by Andrew Smith Hallidie in 1871. This day honors the ingenuity behind these iconic vehicles, which revolutionized urban transportation by efficiently moving passengers up steep inclines using a continuously moving underground cable. Primarily associated with San Francisco, cable cars remain a beloved and historic mode of public transit, celebrated for their unique engineering and nostalgic charm.

Observed each year on January 17, Cable Car Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

San Francisco's cable car system is the last manually operated one of its kind in the world.

The first successful cable car line, the Clay Street Hill Railroad, began operating in San Francisco on August 2, 1873.
