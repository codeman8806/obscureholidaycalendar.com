Overview

Make Up Your Mind Day, observed on December 31st, is dedicated to encouraging individuals to finally settle on choices they've been pondering throughout the year. It's a day to reflect on unresolved decisions, find clarity, and move forward with conviction as a fresh start approaches. Whether big or small, this day champions the art of decisive action.

Observed each year on December 31, Make Up Your Mind Day invites people to pause, share the story, and bring a little themed joy to their day.

Origin and story

The concept of 'analysis paralysis' describes the state of overthinking a decision to the point of never making one.

Fun facts

  • The concept of 'analysis paralysis' describes the state of overthinking a decision to the point of never making one.
  • Studies suggest that making decisions earlier in the day, when mental energy is typically higher, can lead to better outcomes.
  • Procrastination in decision-making is often linked to fear of failure or the desire for perfect information, which is rarely attainable.
  • Breaking down complex decisions into smaller, manageable steps can make the process less daunting and more achievable.
  • Making a decision, even an imperfect one, often provides more relief and opportunities for learning than remaining in indecision.
  • The act of writing down pros and cons can be a powerful tool to visualize and evaluate choices.
